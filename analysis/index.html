It's Official:
Ghosts Are Coming Back

After I finished all 3 books in the Emily Wilde Series by Heather Fawcett in just two days, I found myself in that particular kind of book hangover where you don't want another story — you want more of the same feeling. Cozy libraries, faerie lore, academic heroines, and a slow-burn romance. Curious to learn more about fantasy books, I found a great API — Hardcover.app — and pulled 1,000 books to find out what's really in them: which creatures show up, how readers feel about them, and which ones travel together

01 — Creature mentions over time

Who Shows Up and When

% of books per decade whose description mentions each creature. Dot size = books sampled that decade. Hover a line to isolate it.

Before the 20th century, ghost and vampire stories dominated. By the late 1900s, the creature landscape diversified — elves, dragons, witches, and wizards entered the mainstream. Vampires carried their popularity into the 21st century, while ghosts, long overlooked by writers, have quietly staged a comeback over the past five years.
